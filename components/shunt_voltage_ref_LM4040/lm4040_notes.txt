=== PICO    RP2040  ====================================

Circuit Connections LM4040 
 Pin 1 (Cathode): Connects to the 5V source through the bias resistor.
  Also connect directly to the ADC_VREF pin on the Pi Pico.
 Pin 3 (Anode): Connects to AGND (Analog Ground, Pin 33) on the Pi Pico. 
   Connecting this to the dedicated analog ground minimizes digital noise.
 Pico ADC Pins: Connect your analog sensors to GPIO 26-28 (ADC0-ADC2).

How to Size the Bias Resistor

To calculate the ideal resistor, you use Ohm's law alongside the 
LM4040's operational parameters:
           V_in  -  V_zener
 R_bias = --------------------------
           I_zener + I_load

V_in  5V  ref voltage
V_zener  3.0v regulated voltage provided by LM4040-3.0
I_zener  min operating current for LM4040 is 60 micro-amps
           Prefer 100 micro-amps for stability
I_load   Pico ADC_VREF draws about 150 micro-amps 
           (It actually varies as A-to-D conversion occurs)
Recommended: 1k resistor
  This provides abouth 2 mA of shunt current from a 5V source which keeps
     LM4040 in its operating range and allows overhead for Pico's
     ADC draw.

          │

=== PICO 2   RP2350  ====================================

Unlike the original Pico, the Pico 2 W natively stabilizes its internal 
ADC power rails, but using the LM4040-3.0 precision voltage reference still 
provides excellent stability for highly accurate measurements.

Circuit is the same as above, but slightly difference values.

  5V Reference Source
          │
          ▼
     Bias Resistor (R1) ➔ [ 820Ω ] (For optimal stability)
          │
          ├───┬─────────────────────────▶ Pico 2 W ADC_VREF (Pin 35)
          │   │
          │   ▼
     [ LM4040-3.0 ]  (Pin 1: Cathode, Pin 3: Anode)
          │   ▲
          │   │
          └───┴─────────────────────────▶ Pico 2 W AGND (Pin 33)

The RP2350 ADC input impedance and current draw characteristics shift 
during sampling. To prevent voltage droop when the internal Successive 
Approximation Register (SAR) ADC switches its internal capacitor arrays, 
you must calculate a slightly stiffer shunt current.

Voltage drop across R_bias:
         R_bias = V_in - V_out = 5v - 3v = 2v

By Ohms law: I_bias is 2.4 Ma.
This satisfies both the minimum 150 micro-amp internal ADC switching load 
transients and the optimal 1 mA to 2 mA operating range of the LM4040 
for the lowest dynamic impedance:

                     2.0V
        R_bias = ---------------------------
                    0.0024 amps
               = approx 833 ohms
Standard value: use 820 or 750 ohms.

Power dissipation:  P = I**2 * R  = 4mW so 1/8W or 1/4W resistor is ok.

Pico 2 W config:
 Isolate AGND (Pin 33): Connect Pin 3 (Anode) of the LM4040 directly to 
    Pin 33 (AGND). Do not tie this specific ground track into the 
    noisy digital system ground (GND pins) until they meet at the 
    single-point star ground layout on the board.
  Add a Bypass Capacitor: While the Pico 2 W handles power filtering better,
    adding a 100 nF ceramic capacitor directly across the LM4040 Cathode and 
    Anode pins suppresses high-frequency noise introduced by the onboard 
    wireless Wi-Fi chip transmissions.

Micropython notes:
The RP2350 ADC features a 12-bit resolution 4096 discrete steps. 
Use this formula in your MicroPython / C++ scripts to translate 
raw ADC readings into accurate voltages:

                        RAW ADC value
     actual volatage = -----------------  times 3  (3 volts)
                          4095
